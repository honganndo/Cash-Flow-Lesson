Cash Flow Statement Example

  • Free Cash Flow

    Free cash flow = Operating cash flow - capital expenditures from investments

    In this example, that would be the net cash from operations minus the equipment cost.

    2,012,000 - 500,000 = 1,512,000

  • Operating Cash Flow Ratio

    The operating cash flow ratio represents the company's ability to pay its debts with existing cash flows. It is determined by dividing operating cash flow by current liabilities, the latter of which can be found on a different financial statement, the balance sheet'

    Here the operating cash flow is 2,012,000

  • Implications for Business Operations:

    Positive operating cash flow means that core operations are generating business and that there is enough money to buy new inventory.
